### Detailed Analysis: USA.gov Benefits Experience (October 2024)

**Project Background:**
- Transitioned from Benefits.gov closure to USA.gov
- Major redesign of benefit finder tool
- Comprehensive migration of 1,500+ pages

**Product Team Structure:**
- Product managers
- User research & experience specialists
- Content strategists
- Developers

**Key Implementation Steps:**
1. **Content Audit**: Exhaustive review of Benefits.gov
2. **Data Analysis**: Studied both Benefits.gov and USA.gov data to prioritize topics
3. **Content Strategy**: Combined newly-created materials with existing content
4. **Redirect Plan**: Comprehensive mapping of 1,500+ pages (English + Spanish)
5. **Benefit Finder Redesign**: New look and expanded functionality

**UX Testing Methodology:**
- **Comparative usability testing**: Participants completed tasks on both old and new platforms
- **Iterative improvements**: Based on direct user feedback
- **Improvements made**:
  - Wording optimization
  - Topic order refinement
  - Content structure enhancement
  - **Category filter** added to streamline information by specific types

**Ongoing Work:**
- Studying user data continuously
- Investigating personalization ideas
- Expanding ways to connect people with benefits

**Key Takeaway for Our Project:**
> "Help users find benefits more quickly" through category filtering and streamlined navigation

## ðŸ“± Critical Finding: Mobile-First Design (Code for America, 2019)

### Shocking Statistics:
- **Only 1 in 3** online benefits applications are mobile responsive
- **78%** of American adults own smartphones (vs 73% desktop/laptop)
- **20%** of households rely ONLY on smartphones (up from 8% in 2013)
- **31%** of low-income adults ($30k/year) exclusively use smartphones for internet
- **50%+** of Google searches for food assistance are from mobile devices

### User Quote:
> "My phone _is_ my computer." â€” Client in Colorado

### Common Mobile UX Problems Found:

**1. Desktop Layouts Become Unreadable**
- Text shrinks to minuscule fonts
- Content extends off screen edges
- Requires excessive scrolling
- On-screen keyboards cause layout issues

**2. Desktop Interactions Don't Translate**
- Hover states don't work on touch
- Dropdown menus fail
- Click targets too small
- Forms difficult to complete

**3. Excessive Vertical Scrolling**
- Dozens of questions at once
- Users feel fatigued/overwhelmed
- Hard to find questions below fold
